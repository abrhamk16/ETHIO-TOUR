History & Culture

Ancient History: Ethiopia has one of the oldest and richest histories in the world, starting from early human origins. Fossils like “Lucy” found in the Afar region show that some of the earliest humans lived there millions of years ago. Later, one of the first civilizations, the Kingdom of Dʿmt, developed around 1000 BCE and built impressive structures such as the temple at Yeha. After that, the Aksumite Empire rose to power and became a major trading center connecting Africa, Arabia, and Asia. It was known for its tall obelisks, coins, and strong economy. Under King Ezana, Aksum adopted Christianity in the 4th century, which influenced Ethiopian culture deeply.

Modern Legacy: Ethiopia’s modern history began in the late 19th century under Emperor Menelik II, who expanded and unified the country. In 1896, Ethiopia defeated Italy in the Battle of Adwa, preserving its independence. Later, Italy invaded again in 1935, leading to a brief occupation until 1941 when Emperor Haile Selassie was restored to power. Haile Selassie worked to modernize the country and played a key role in founding the Organization of African Unity in 1963.

Coffee Ceremony

Ethiopian coffee is famous worldwide and is believed to have originated in the region of Kaffa, where the coffee plant was first discovered.

13 Months

The Ethiopian calendar, used in Ethiopia, has 13 months of 30 days each. The 13th month, called Pagume, has 5 days or 6 days in a leap year.

Ethiopic Ge'ez

Geʽez is an ancient language that was once widely spoken but is now mainly used in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church.
